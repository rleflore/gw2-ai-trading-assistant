# GW2 Economy Rules

## Trading Post Fees

The Trading Post (TP) charges two fees on every sale:

- **Listing Fee (5%):** Deducted from the seller's wallet immediately when listing an item.
- **Sale Fee (10%):** Deducted from the sale revenue when the item sells.
- **Total cost to sell: 15%.** This applies whether you list an item or sell instantly to a buy order.
- **Buying has no fee.** The buyer pays only the listed price.

For a trade to be profitable after fees, the sell price must exceed the buy price by more than 17.6% (1 / 0.85).

## Buy and Sell Orders

Each item on the TP shows two columns:

- **Buy orders (left):** The highest price a buyer is willing to pay. Sellers can "sell instantly" to this.
- **Sell listings (right):** The lowest price a seller is asking. Buyers can "buy instantly" at this price.

Player behavior:
- Most players list items rather than selling instantly.
- Undercutting the lowest seller by 1 copper is standard practice.
- Items under ~1 gold are often sold instantly because the margin from listing is too small to justify waiting.
- Exotic rarity and above items are almost always listed rather than sold instantly.

## Vendor Value

Every item has a fixed vendor value (shown below its name in the TP). Items that cannot be vendored show zero. This sets a hard price floor — no rational seller would list below vendor value.

## Gold-Gem Exchange

Players can convert gold to gems (cash shop currency) and vice versa. The exchange rate is player-driven.

- Gnashblade (the exchange operator) skims approximately 17-18% of both gold and gems per transaction.
- Rate spikes occur during: Black Lion Chest updates, key discounts, new gliders/wings, gem store sales, new outfits/mounts, and seasonal events.
- Wintersday often sees a spike (new items) followed by a dip (players spending real-money gift cards on gold).

## Gold Sinks

Major gold sinks that remove currency from the economy:

- Trading Post fees (15% on every transaction)
- Mystic Forge recipes requiring vendor-purchased components
- Waypoint costs
- Legendary crafting (Mystic Runestones at 1g each, Icy Runestones at 1g each)
- Commander tags, guild upgrades
- Black Lion Trading Company convenience items

## Material Sinks

The Mystic Forge is the primary material sink:

- Mystic Clover gambling consumes Mystic Coins, Ectos, Obsidian Shards
- Material promotion (lower tier → higher tier) consumes large quantities
- Precursor gambling ("the Toilet") destroys four items for one output
- Ecto gambling at Tarrktun destroys Globs of Ectoplasm (net gold sink)

## Time-Gated Crafting

Certain ascended material refinements can only be crafted once per day per account:

- Lump of Mithrillium (metal)
- Glob of Elder Spirit Residue (cloth)
- Spool of Thick Elonian Cord (leather)
- Spiritwood Plank (wood)

These require Tier 5 basic materials + Globs of Ectoplasm. The daily gate creates consistent demand and limits supply, keeping ascended material prices elevated. The refined outputs (Deldrimor Steel Ingot, Bolt of Damask, Elonian Leather Square, Spiritwood Plank) are tradeable on the TP.

## Wizard Vault

The Wizard Vault replaced daily login rewards. Players earn Astral Acclaim by completing daily, weekly, and seasonal objectives.

Popular purchases that affect the economy:
- Legendary Weapon Starter Kit (Gift + Precursor + Gift of Magic/Might choice)
- Mystic Coins (steady supply injection)
- Guaranteed Mystic Clovers (bypasses gambling)
- Gold
- Obsidian Shards
- Ascended Weapon Chests

The Wizard Vault provides a steady drip of materials into the economy, putting downward pressure on Mystic Coin and Clover prices over time.

## Seasonal Events & Price Patterns

Annual events create predictable demand spikes:

- **Wintersday (December):** Snowflakes, Wintersday gifts spike. Ecto often drops due to reduced player activity or supply flooding.
- **Halloween (October):** Trick-or-Treat Bags, Candy Corn spike.
- **Festival of the Four Winds (August):** Globs of Ectoplasm spike heavily — used to gamble on Zephyrite Supply Boxes for rare infusions.
- **Dragon Bash:** Relevant consumables and materials spike.
- **SAB (Super Adventure Box):** Baubles and related items see movement.

Pattern: Event materials spike 1-3 days before the event starts (speculation) and often crash during the event as supply floods in.
